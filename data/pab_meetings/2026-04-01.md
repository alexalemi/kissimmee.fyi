# Planning Advisory Board Meeting Summary
## April 1, 2026

**Meeting Time:** 6:00 PM - 6:32 PM

---

## Meeting Overview

The Planning Advisory Board convened for a regular meeting with two Land Development Code text amendments on the agenda. The board rejected a Commission-directed proposal to allow finance, insurance, and real estate (FIRE) offices on ground floors in downtown Kissimmee, finding it inconsistent with the comprehensive plan. A second item regarding conditional use review authority was continued after the City Attorney's Office disclosed it had not completed its review. The meeting also included a brief recess to observe the Artemis II space shuttle launch.

## Agenda Items

### 5.A Public Hearing - Proposed Ordinance #26-10 - LDC Text Amendment (Sections 14-5-3 and 14-5-6: FIRE Offices on Ground Floors Downtown)

#### Background

Senior Planner Ashley Cornelison presented the item, which had two components:

1. **Map correction:** Restoring a portion of Figure 5-25 that was inadvertently left out during the last LDC update.
2. **Substantive amendment:** Allowing finance, insurance, and real estate (FIRE) offices on the first floor of units fronting Broadway and connected side streets in downtown.

Cornelison provided historical context: In 1998, a stakeholder committee reviewed the B-1 (Downtown Commercial) zoning district and recommended prohibiting all office uses on ground floors fronting Broadway to promote a human-scaled, pedestrian-active downtown. Those restrictions were adopted in 1999, expanded with the Form-Based Code in 2020 to include side streets, and further supported by the road diet reducing Broadway from four lanes to two.

In December 2025, the City Commission directed staff to remove the restriction specifically for FIRE offices. Staff recommended approval based on this directive.

#### Board Discussion

**Board Member Alemi** raised comprehensive plan consistency concerns, citing two policies:
- **Policy 1.1.6.5** - Encourages vertical mixed-use buildings within the multimodal transportation district, stating "lower floors should have more public uses with more private uses on the upper-level floors"
- **Policy 1.1.6.3** - Requires pedestrian-oriented building design within the multimodal transportation district, with transparency, articulation, and features of visual interest to pedestrians

Alemi argued that FIRE uses are inherently more private than public and that permitting them on ground floors would fail the four-part test required under LDC Section 14-3-27(c) for text amendments, particularly the requirement for general consistency with the comprehensive plan. He also noted a letter from the Downtown Kissimmee Council (DKC) of the Osceola Chamber, which opposed the broad change but offered a compromise: allow FIRE offices only in buildings that already lack pedestrian-oriented frontages.

**Board Member Arguello** questioned why the city would selectively permit only FIRE offices rather than opening ground floors to all offices, since all office types have a similar impact on pedestrian traffic. Staff responded that the scope was limited to the Commission's directive.

**Board Member Sanchez** expressed concern that the amendment was inconsistent with the city's stated goals. He argued that selectively permitting specific business categories "creates a lobbying environment" where industries seek carve-outs, which "no city benefits from." He suggested the city should reconcile its comprehensive plan goals before amending the code.

**Chairman Bussiere** offered a counterpoint, citing real estate offices on Park Avenue in Winter Park as examples that complement walkable downtowns, noting that "it's not necessarily an eyesore." He disclosed that he is a licensed realtor but has no interest in opening office space downtown.

**Board Member Lopez** seconded the motion and voted in favor.

#### Public Comments

None.

- **Motion to Approve:** Alex Alemi (procedural motion to bring to a vote under Robert's Rules)
- **Second:** Javin Lopez
- **Roll Call Vote:**
  - AYE: Bussiere, Lopez

### 5.B Public Hearing - Proposed Ordinance #26-11 - LDC Text Amendment (Sections 14-3-16 and 14-3-29: Conditional Use Review Authority)

This ordinance proposed significant changes to conditional use regulations:
- Adding the **City Commission as the final reviewing authority** for conditional uses (currently PAB has final authority)
- Adding standards for **site plan waivers** allowing conceptual plans instead of full site plans
- Adding **time frames** on conditional use approvals to allow periodic re-review

Before any discussion, **Assistant City Attorney Curtis McGehee** informed the board that due to a miscommunication between Development Services and the City Attorney's Office, the City Attorney's Office had not conducted its typical legal review of the item. He recommended the board take no action, noting that legal review could change the content of the ordinance, making discussion of the current version potentially unproductive.

- **Motion to Continue:** Raymond Sanchez
- **Second:** Alex Alemi
- **Result:** Passed 5-0

## Key Takeaways

1. **PAB rejected the Commission's FIRE office directive:** A majority of board members found the proposal inconsistent with the comprehensive plan's pedestrian-oriented goals, despite it being a direct Commission directive. This sets up a potential tension when the item goes to Commission.

2. **Conditional use authority change delayed:** The proposed shift of conditional use final authority from PAB to City Commission — a significant reduction in PAB's power — was continued before any discussion due to incomplete legal review.

3. **DKC compromise not adopted:** The Downtown Kissimmee Council's suggestion to limit FIRE offices to buildings without pedestrian-friendly frontages was discussed but no amended motion incorporating it was made.

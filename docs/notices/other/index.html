PUBLIC NOTICE PLEASE TAKE NOTICE that Osceola County hereby offers for sell or lease, on a continual basis, various parcels of real property owned by the County in NeoCity, a 483-acre technology district located south of E. Irlo Bronson Memorial Highway across from Osceola Heritage Park, for development consistent with the purposes described in the NeoCity Master Plan dated June 12, 2017. Persons offering to purchase or lease property in NeoCity must demonstrate that the proposed use of the property will stimulate economic growth within the County by growing high-wage and high-value added employment opportunities, and especially by promoting economic diversification, high-technology research and innovation, and fostering a vibrant advanced sensor and other advanced manufacturing industry in the County. Terms of any sale or lease will depend on the location and quantity of real property and other factors, including the economic benefit to be derived by the County from the proposed use of the property, and are subject to review and approval by the Board of County Commissioners. Use of property sold or leased is subject to covenants, conditions and restrictions applicable to the particular property; and standard or negotiated sale or lease terms approved by the County. Parties interested in entering into negotiations for purchase or lease of particular parcels should contact the Osceola County Economic Development Department, 1 Courthouse Square, Suite 4300, Kissimmee, FL 34741, Phone: (407) 742-4207, Email: economicdevelopment@osceola.org [economicdevelopment@osceola.org] Should any party and the County agree to the terms of any proposed sale or lease, the proposed sale or lease agreement will be scheduled for recommended approval by the Board or County Commissioners. All persons registered with the Osceola County Economic Development Department, as described below, will receive emailed notice of the meeting date. Any other person wishing to submit an offer on the particular parcel(s) upon which a recommended sale or lease has been submitted to the Board must propose in writing, and deliver to the Osceola County Economic Development Department, sale or lease and use terms which are more advantageous to the County before the date and time set by Board agenda for action on the recommended sale or lease under consideration. If the Board timely receives multiple offers for sale or lease for the same parcel(s) of land, then the Board, in its sole legislative discretion, will determine which offer for sale or lease, if any, is the most advantageous to the County. The Board of County Commissioners further reserves the right to reject any and all offer(s) for sale or lease upon determination that such offer(s) is (are) not in the best interests of the County as determined solely in the exercise of the legislative discretion vested in the Board under the Constitution and laws of the State of Florida. Interested persons who wish to register their names and email addresses to be advised of Board agendas which include sales or leases of property at NeoCity should contact the Osceola County Economic Development Department. RIGHT TO REQUEST ADMINISTRATIVE HEARING A person whose substantial interests are or may be affected by the South Florida Water Management District's (District) action has the right to request an administrative hearing on that action pursuant to Sections 120.569 and 120.57, Florida Statutes. Persons seeking a hearing on District decision which affects or may affect their substantial interests shall file a petition for hearing in accordance with the filing instructions set forth herein within 21 days of receipt of written notice of the decision unless one of the following shorter time periods apply: (1) within 14 days of the notice of consolidated intent to grant or deny concurrently reviewed applications for environmental resource permits and use of sovereign submerged lands pursuant to Section 373.427, Florida Statutes; or (2) within 14 days of service of an Administrative Order pursuant to Section 373.119(1), Florida Statutes. "Receipt of written notice of agency decision" means receipt of written notice through mail, electronic mail, posting, or publication that the District has taken or intends to take final agency action. Any person who receives written notice of a District decision and fails to file a written request for hearing within the timeframe described above waives the right to request a hearing on that decision. If the District takes final agency action that materially differs from the noticed intended agency decision, persons who may be substantially affected shall, unless otherwise provided by law, have an additional point of entry pursuant to Rule 28-106.111, Florida Administrative Code. Any person to whom an emergency order is directed pursuant to Section 373.119(2), Florida Statutes, shall comply therewith immediately, but on petition to the board shall be afforded a hearing as soon as possible. A person may file a request for an extension of time for filing a petition. The District may grant the request for good cause. Requests for extension of time must be filed with the District prior to the deadline for filing a petition for hearing. Such requests for extension shall contain a certificate that the moving party has consulted with all other parties concerning the extension and whether the District and any other parties agree to or oppose the extension. A timely request for an extension of time shall toll the running of the time period for filing a petition until the request is acted upon.
